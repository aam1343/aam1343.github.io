-You can never put too much pork in your mouth as far as I'm concerned.- Lewis Black

Pigs are probably the most practical animals to raise and butcher from a small-scale agricultural standpoint. They were initially domesticated because of their functionality in sustaining families in both life and death.

In life they are recyclers of sorts; omnivores able to sustain themselves on anything. As sustenance they are nose-to-tail animals; you can utilize every single part of a pig, rendering the fat, frying the skin, smoking the hocks, stuffing the intestines, curing the loin, among other parts and forms of preparation.

Culinary arts has found numerous ways, over the course of history, to make masterpieces out of the humble pig. If you are a pork eater, I am sure pulled pork, chops, and bacon are all things you are familiar with. Feeling adventurous? I recommend:

  • -Grilled Pigs Feet
  • -Braised Pork Jowls
